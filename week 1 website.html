At dawn I was awoken by a flicker of light. A small arm retreated from the entrance to my cave. Some sentry had registered my presence. He was hoping I would register his. I turned away, fearful of confrontation, too weak to heed any call. My sleep was fitful, though, and in time he returned, and, in a tiny voice of urgency, summoned me up from my cave. I stirred slowly. I shook the crust from my eyes. It was no hour to be moving. But I knew the reward was immeasurable. I was here for this fruit and this small sentry knew its location better than anyone. Could I trust his intentions? Could you?

I reviewed what I knew about the fruit:

  • It is a precious fruit
  • It produces feelings of total and ineffable warmth
  • It is a shapeshifter, never appearing the same in two photographs taken in consecutive days
  • It assumes the form of a leaping frog, or of a dense yam
  • It smells like sweet air except for when it smells like an off egg
  • It is better held than eaten
  • It can make a sound
  • It can be soundless
  • It can be yours or mine
